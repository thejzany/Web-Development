Freedom Fighters

Name: Bhagat Singh

         Bhagat Singh is considered one of the most influential nationalist leaders of the Indian Independence movement. Bhagat Singh is often referred to as ‘Shaheed’ Bhagat Singh. The word ‘Shaheed’ means a martyr. “If the deaf are to hear, the sound has to be very loud. When we dropped the bomb, it was not our intention to kill anybody, we have bombed the British Government, British must quit India and make it free.” Bhagat Singh said this after the Assembly Bombing.

  • Achievements
    1. Joining the Hindustan Republican Association (HRA) at a young age and becoming involved in revolutionary activities
    2. Participating in several acts of sabotage against British institutions, including an attempt to bomb the Central Legislative Assembly in Delhi
    3. Being convicted of assassinating John Saunders, a British police officer, for which he was hanged at the age of 23

    Name: Chandra Shekar Azad

            An Indian Revolutionary leader and Freedom fighter, Chandra Shekhar Tiwari was better known by his stage name Chandrashekhar Azad. He reorganized the Hindustan Republican Association (HRA) under the new name of Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA) following the passing of the organization’s founder, Ram Prasad Bismil , as well as three other important party figures, Roshan Singh, Rajendra Nath Lahiri, and Ashfaqulla Khan. He frequently used the alias “Balraj” when signing pamphlets as the leader of the HSRA

  • Achievements
    1. Active member of the Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA).
    2. Participation in the Kakori Conspiracy of 1925.
    3. Mastery of disguise to evade British authorities.

      4. Name: Veer Savarkar

              In the annals of India’s struggle for independence, few names command the same fervour a Veer Savarkar. An enigmatic figure who donned various roles as a revolutionary, freedom fighter, writer, and political leader,Vinayak Damodar Savarkars, popularly known as Veer Savarkar, left an indelible mark on the country’s history.

    4. Achievements
      1. Advocated for complete independence (Swaraj) from British rule.
      2. Wrote influential books and poems on nationalism and Hindutva.
      3. Coined the term "First War of Independence" for the 1857 uprising.
