Chai

"Chai" typically refers to a type of tea, specifically a spiced tea that originated in India. It is also known as "masala chai," where "masala" means a mixture of spices. Chai is made by brewing black tea with a combination of aromatic spices and herbs, usually including ingredients like black tea leaves, cardamom, cinnamon, ginger, cloves, and black peppercorns. The exact blend of spices can vary depending on regional preferences and individual recipes.

Ingredents

  • 1 Cup Water
  • 2 Tea Spoons Tea leaves
  • Sugar, to taste
  • 1 Cup Milk

Recipe

  1. In a pot, combine 1 cup of water with a few spices like cardamom, cinnamon, cloves, and a small piece of crushed ginger.
  2. Bring the water and spices to a boil. This will infuse the water with the spices' flavors.
  3. Add 2-3 teaspoons of black tea leaves or 1-2 tea bags to the boiling water. Pour in 1 cup of milk (whole milk or a milk substitute of your choice).
  4. Reduce the heat to low and let the chai simmer for 5-10 minutes. Simmering allows the flavors to meld.
  5. Add sugar to taste (usually 1-2 teaspoons) and stir until dissolved. Strain the chai into cups and enjoy your homemade chai!
